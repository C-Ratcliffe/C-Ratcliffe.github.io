Music Recommendations

Artists and projects I'd like others to know about

Recommendations are made purely based on my own subjective listening experience.
There will be a heavy focus on extreme metal artists, although I will try to be as eclectic as possible.
Artists are grouped by genre umbrella, so there will undoubtedly be some level of subjectivity involved. Unfortunately, I can't please everyone.

Avante-garde Metal:

  • Igorrr: Alias and project of French musician, Gautier Serre, Igorrr is a ludicrous combination of baroque, EDM, and black metal.

Black Metal:

  • Zeal and Ardor: A Swiss six-piece, playing gospel and blues infused black metal.

Death Metal:

  • 1914: A Ukrainian five-piece, whose blackened-death metal is as heavy as the WWI themed lyrical focus.

Folk Metal:

  • Amon Amarth: One of the largest names in folk metal, Amon Amarth are a Swedish five-piece famous for their groovy, norse mythology themed take on death metal.

Grind and Noise:

  • Anaal Nathrakh: Legendary Birmingham (UK) two-piece grindcore outfit Anaal Nathrakh are the auditory equIvalent of being hit by a bus.

Hard Rock:

  • Ghost: Tobias Forge's hard rock project, Ghost, combine blues, doom, and camp aethetics to put out some of the catchiest rock hits ever written.

Industrial:

  • Rammstein: Legendary neue Deutsche h√§rte pioneers, Rammstein, are provocative, dramatic, and unsurpassed in the industrial scene.

Piano Rock and Acoustic Comedy:

  • Tim Minchin: Marrying comedy and piano rock, Tim Minchin's music is a celebration of both, with a wit that stays on the accessible side of obnoxious.

Power Metal:

  • Sabaton: Sabaton are a Swedish five-piece who put out catchy (if somewhat cheesy) heavy metal that juxtaposes occassionally bleak historical themes with modern melodic metal trappings.

Rave and Hyperpop:

  • Little Big: This tongue-and-cheek Russian project are responsible for some irresistably catchy rave tracks that celebrate slavic culture, whilst criticising the prevalence of systemic abuse.

All opinions presented are solely my own
Recommendations of media and resources are made independent of any ideologies/beliefs those involved in the production may hold
